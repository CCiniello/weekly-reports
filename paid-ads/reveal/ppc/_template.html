Demand Gen is running broadly against a single generic audience, and nearly all of its traffic is directed to the homepage — where engagement (34%) runs well below paid search (58%) and bounce is high. It is buying reach, but not yet the intent-level quality the program needs.

The suggestion is to segment Demand Gen by intent so spend follows the strongest signal and each tier can be measured on quality: a top tier of competitor and pricing searchers, a middle tier of embedded-analytics and BI researchers, and a broad awareness tier. Each tier should point to a dedicated, matching page — comparison and pricing pages for high intent — rather than the homepage. This turns Demand Gen into a graded quality-and-reach engine instead of a single broad line.

4e · Quality Score — the unlock

Diagnosis (keyword-level data): across every Non-Brand-US ad group, landing-page experience is "Below average" on 100% of keywords and predicted CTR is "Below average" almost everywhere, while ad relevance is mostly "Above average." The ads are on-target; the pages and the copy hooks are what pull ad rank down. Because Google won't serve a low-QS ad even when we'll pay, this is exactly why the flagship budget sits idle — and raising Quality Score lifts rank without raising bids.

Landing-page experience — the fixes (this is the bigger lever)
  • Build the two missing pages (/embedded-bi, /embedded-dashboards). Those ad groups have no dedicated page, so the score is capped until they exist.
  • Repoint every ad to its exact-match page (not the homepage) and make the H1 mirror the keyword.
  • Improve the existing pages (/embedded-analytics, /white-label-analytics): page speed & Core Web Vitals, mobile experience, clear above-the-fold value, a visible demo/CTA, and deeper on-topic content. Landing-page experience is more than message match — speed and relevance both count.
Predicted CTR — the second lever
  • Refresh the ad copy (RSA): pin the exact keyword into headlines, sharpen CTAs, add sitelinks/callouts. Suggestions are in the monthly copy refresh (§11).
  • Leave ad relevance alone — it's already "Above average," so effort there is wasted; and don't raise bids blindly, because the constraint is Quality Score, not bid.

This placement is underperforming, and it's a setup problem — not a channel problem. Over the flight the sponsored ad drew 67 impressions and 0 clicks; the free organic listing outperformed it. Three misconfigurations explain it:

  • No competitors targeted. The placement is category-only, so it never appears on the competitor and "alternatives" pages where buyers actually shortlist — the same Power BI / Tableau / Sisense comparisons where our search demand is surging.
  • The CTA points to the homepage, not a comparison or embedded-analytics page — the wrong destination for a shortlisting buyer.
  • Volume is minimal (67 impressions in ~12 weeks) because the lone category page carries little traffic.
The fix
  • Add targeted competitors (Power BI, Tableau, Sisense, Metabase, Luzmo, Looker, Domo, GoodData); lead with Power BI given the live demand.
  • Repoint the CTA to /reveal-vs-<competitor> and /embedded-analytics.
  • Evaluate G2 Clicks (CPC) for accountability, and harvest G2 buyer-intent into ABM / 6sense. Live intent pull is one connector approval away.

11 Scaling — how do we grow quality volume?

Can we 10x paid volume? Not on paid search alone. This is a high-ticket, niche category; total high-intent search volume caps pure capture. The realistic near-term prize is ~1.5-2x quality leads over a quarter by unlocking what's already wasted, then adding surfaces. 10x is a portfolio question — new channels plus more mid-funnel — not a bid change.
