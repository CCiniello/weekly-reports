Executive summary

the one thing: leads up, quality flat

The single most important thing this week: paid Reveal leads rose to 17 (+70% WoW) on essentially flat spend, pulling blended cost-per-paid-lead down to ~$606 from ~$892. That's the third straight week of an upward lead trend (12-week series is climbing). But the gains are happening in spite of three unfixed leaks, not because they're solved: brand-term impression share is stuck at ~58% (we should own ~100% of our own name), Google Non-Brand-US keywords sit at Quality Score 3 with "below average" landing-page experience because the two core pages don't exist yet, and 67% of 90-day paid leads are Sales-Unqualified with LeadQuality__c still empty, so we cannot yet prove the mix is improving.

Money is being allocated roughly right — Google search + DemandGen are carrying demand capture, LinkedIn's image ad is the ROI driver, Reddit is holding awareness cheaply. The waste is small but fixable: ~$106 of Bing spend went to junk queries again this week (toucan, insolence, grok — negatives still not live), and LinkedIn retargeting burned $210 for a single click on a starved audience. Fix brand IS and the two landing pages and the same spend produces materially more qualified pipeline.

Demand Gen read

reaching in-market, or leaking?

DemandGen is a top-3 spend line ($1,354 Compete-US + $954 Non-Brand-US + $940 Compete-Intl + $508 NB-Intl ≈ $3.76k, ~46% of Google spend) but returned 0–1 tracked conversions this week against 129k impressions. CTRs are weak (0.67–1.16%), which is normal for DemandGen prospecting, but the campaigns are currently broad audiences pointed largely at the homepage — the exact combination that leaks. It is reaching volume, not intent.

  • Rebuild by intent tiers: T1 custom-intent from the surging competitor/pricing terms → comparison & pricing pages; T2 embedded-analytics/BI researchers + site visitors; T3 broad (small, capped).
  • Point T1–T2 at /reveal-vs-* and /pricing/embedded-analytics (which engage 54–75% in GA4) instead of homepage (34%).
  • PMAX-Remarketing-WW spent $1,160 for 0 conversions on 25k impr — audit its placements/asset mix next week.

Do not starve non-brand capture on last-click leads alone; the 90-day pipeline is the truer read of paid's contribution.
